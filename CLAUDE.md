# Knight Stained Glass - Website Project

## Project Overview
Custom-built website for **Andrea Knight**, owner of **Knight Stained Glass** in the Cincinnati, OH area. Andrea is a stained glass artist specializing in church restorations, commercial work, residential repairs, and custom designs.

## Client Profile
- **Name:** Andrea Knight
- **Business:** Knight Stained Glass
- **Location:** Cincinnati, OH area
- **Logo:** Shield with a medieval cross - plays on "Knight" surname and church connection
- **Google presence:** 37 five-star reviews, ranks well for "Cincinnati stained glass"
- **Competition:** Willett (national company, studio in Westchester), BeauVerre
- **Current major project:** Notre Dame church restoration


## Service Categories
Andrea's work breaks into three categories that the site must clearly communicate:
1. **Religious / Church** - Large-scale church restorations, ecclesiastical work. This is her differentiator - she has more restoration experience than anyone else in Cincinnati. Needs to be prominent.
2. **Commercial** - Hotels, businesses, etc. (e.g., Moxy Hotel project, Stone Mad Cleveland)
3. **Residential** - Front doors, bathroom windows, sun catchers, beveled glass repair. Smaller jobs, homeowner-friendly.

## Website Requirements

### Core Goals
- Communicate experience and professionalism immediately - especially for church work
- Make it dead simple for anyone (church secretary, construction foreman, homeowner) to find what they need
- Dark background preferred (colors pop better against black)
- Medieval / iron / old-world aesthetic that leans into the Knight brand and centuries-old craft
- Mobile-first - most visitors on phones, scrolling is cheap

### Structure
- **Single page preferred** with scroll navigation (not separate pages for each section)
- Header/nav can scroll to sections
- Sections: Church/Religious, Commercial, Residential, About/Bio, Contact
- Bio with personal photos (dog photo has landed clients, also scaffolding/work photos)
- The word "church restorations" needs to be prominent and visible

### Design Direction
- Dark background, light/luminous content
- Medieval, iron-textured aesthetic
- Interactive light effects on stained glass images (light follows cursor, illumination effects) (maybe)
- Must still be immediately usable for older, less tech-savvy visitors (church people aged 50-80)
- Not "cute" - professional, substantial, experienced

### Technical
- Andrea will NOT update the site herself - periodic paid updates by developer
